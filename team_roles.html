Group Processes and Communications

Open, honest and frank conversations have always been a vital element to the success of Phronesis Collective. To achieve this, twice-weekly meetings were organised to satisfy members scheduling requirements. Meetings were conducted via video recording through MS Teams and were covered by an Agenda and a Chairperson. Discussion in the MS Teams Chat feature was also encouraged.

It was accepted by all members that there would be responsibilities and timeframes placed upon them to achieve the required outcomes. In the event members could not achieve what was expected, it was their responsibility to speak up and advise as soon as it was identified. Teamwork, collaboration and achievements were constantly assessed by the teams Project Manager.

Unfortunately, Assignment Three saw conflict within Phronesis Collective when a new member joined. It became apparent early that the member held differing ideals and values and was not conducive to team cohesion. The original Phronesis Collective team members employed many different conflict management approaches to little avail. The teams Project Manager engaged with University Tutors for advice and the member was soon removed from the team. While this left a hole in the project plan, Phronesis Collective were able to realign with the assignment requirements and pushed forward to achieve the goals.

Phronesis Collective also had one team member who was unavailable to commit to any responsibilities or deadlines. To support them and to achieve the assignment goals, team members agreed to continue in the hopes that they would re-join later.
